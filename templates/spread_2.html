Evolution and Global Spread of Tea

Significant milestones in the history of tea.


The introduction of new processing methods led to the creation of various tea types such as green, black, and oolong. The Ming era marked the beginning of tea's global trade, facilitating interaction with European merchants in the 16th century.

By the 19th century, the British East India Company began cultivating tea in India, effectively breaking China's long-standing monopoly on tea production. This shift not only diversified tea cultivation globally but also altered the economic and cultural landscape of tea.

These developments underscored the dynamic nature of tea's history, as it became a commodity deeply intertwined with global trade and cultural exchange.
