Tang and Song Dynasties: The Global Influence of Chinese Tea

The evolution of tea during significant historical periods.


By the Tang Dynasty (618–907 AD), tea had become an integral part of Chinese society. The period saw the creation of the first tea monograph, "The Classic of Tea" (茶经) by Lu Yu, which elevated tea to an art form and codified its cultivation and consumption.

The expansion of the Silk Road facilitated the first major wave of tea exportation, allowing tea to reach Central Asia and the Middle East.

Moving into the Song Dynasty (960–1279 AD), the development of loose-leaf tea and the invention of powdered tea marked significant advancements. These innovations were introduced to Japan by Buddhist monks, furthering the global influence of Chinese tea.

The historical journey of Chinese tea not only underscores its cultural significance but also highlights its role as a catalyst in early international trade, affecting global tea culture profoundly.
