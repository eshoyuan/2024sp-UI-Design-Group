Historical Origins

Shen Nong and Tea's discovery

Tea's history dates back to 2737 BC

attributed to Emperor Shen Nong(Pic from left), who is said to have discovered tea when leaves from a wild tree blew into his pot of boiling water.

Initially used for medicinal purposes, tea leaves were consumed for their health benefits. The earliest recorded use of tea as a beverage is found in the 3rd century AD in a medical text by Hua Tuo.

Known for its rich aromas, diverse flavors, and deep cultural significance, the history of Chinese tea is rooted in millennia of agricultural craftsmanship and ritual.
The tea culture in China is celebrated for its ceremonial tea practices, including the famed Gongfu tea ceremony, which involves a precise and mindful preparation of tea, symbolizing the Chinese philosophy of "harmony and respect."
China's tea culture not only illustrates its profound historical roots but also highlights its role as a global influencer in tea cultivation and consumption, shaping tea traditions around the world. With its blend of tradition and continuous innovation, Chinese tea remains a cornerstone of social gatherings and a serene companion to personal reflection, appealing to tea lovers globally.
